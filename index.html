      Chord diagrams

      How each diagram is drawn and annotated

      Draws every string in the colours of the Aquila Kids educational ukulele set — green (G), red (C), yellow (E) and blue (A). On other instruments and tunings the colours follow the string positions.
      Chords like 7add9, 6add9 and maj7add9 have 5 notes, but these instruments only have 4 strings — the 5th is dropped. When on, affected chords show a note naming the dropped tone.
      Crop above fret 5

      Diagrams normally start at the nut. If a chord's lowest fretted note is above this fret, the diagram is cropped to a short window at the chord's position instead. Example — a chord played entirely at fret 5:

      Alternative voicings

      How far up the neck voicings are searched and how wide a stretch they may need

      Search up to fret 15
      Highest fret considered when searching for a chord voicing. Raise it to unlock higher positions and more alternative voicings; lower it to keep chords near the nut.
      Max stretch 7
      The widest stretch a fingering may cover, counted in frets from its lowest to its highest fretted note — open strings don't count. Voicings needing a wider reach are discarded: at 7, a voicing using frets 2 and 8 (seven frets) is kept, one using frets 2 and 9 is dropped.
